So what is this article about?

Pokémon Showdown!, known for being a top quality battle simulator, is a wonderful website where there's a lot to do. On your travels around PS!, you'll encounter many people you'd rather not have. There's a lot of variety, but some common archetypes stick out like a sore thumb.

As a staff member, you have the joy to interact with numerous kinds of bad users on the simulator. The nicknames for them are just me being creative, but from the description you'll be able to easily identify some of them when you see them in the chat.

The Bait Quitters

You're just trying to whittle away a couple of hours by laddering, probably hoping to get a higher rating with a decent team you've made. Unfortunately, there are those people on the ladder that try to ruin it for you and everyone else. They usually travel in groups with other friends, and when they all enter at once, you can find it hard to concentrate. This can especially happen when they are trash-talking about your team or your lack of knowledge of the tier, or trying to motivate their friend by spamming in some way.

Nearing the end of the battle, if you are beating the opponent who you are facing with the mass of spectators, some of those people may start typing things like this:

  • SwagMasterBoss ExilineFR you have been caught cheating on the ladder. If you don't wish to get BANNED, please type /concede in the next 30 seconds.

I know it may seem pretty simple to ignore, but you would be surprised by the reports that we get from people doing this; we do take action and proceed to lock the person as this is not to be tolerated at all. If you do see this, please ignore it. If you find spectators annoying in general there is a 'Ignore Spectators' option found in the top right, however you'll have to re-enable every battle. Make sure to continue battling normally and report it to a global staff member. The command /concede was actually removed, as too many people were using it to trick people into forfeiting and it was hardly used anyway.

Shitposters

Everyone is trying to have a nice conversation about a particular Pokémon that might be getting a suspect test, or about one that has become more usable since the ban of another. The point is, it is a reasonable topic filled with people sharing their thoughts. The kind of thing you'd expect to see on, you know, a pokemon simulator.

Shitposters. They contribute towards the chat, fair enough. However, they don't do it in a good way. They lure in the chat and wait for something to argue against. They defend their argument with invalid points that aren't sensible at all. They can turn a reasonable topic bad really quickly. Users tend to get influenced sometimes without realizing it, therefore making the whole chat bad

Format Abusers

There are people who think that the rules don't apply to them. They want to stand out from everyone else and cause a scene in the chat. They usually do this by abusing text formats. Formats such as bold were added to the simulator to get people to stand out, but only in certain circumstances. In the Lobby is not one of them which is why bold is disabled in there. A perfect example is the Tournaments room, where users want to advertise their tournaments. You don't need to draw attention to yourself by abusing capitals or stretching out letters either. You may feel like you won't get noticed in the chat, but you would be surprised by how many people want to join in on the conversation if you contribute positively. Make sure to avoid trouble.

Sore Losers

You're just trying to have a normal ladder battle and everything is going fine. At the end, you're happy with the result because you won, but your opponent may not respond so positively towards it for a simple reason: they lost. Whether it be from a critical hit, an untimely miss, skill, or some other form of luck, they won't be happy. Some people simply say a few things out of rage and then leave before you get a chance to reply.
