[SHORT DESC] User switches, but passes on all stat changes and some special conditions. [LONG DESC]

Description

The user switches with another Pokemon on its team and passes along all stat changes and some special conditions which are listed below. In a rotation battle, the user cannot switch to an inactive teammate on the field using Baton Pass.

The following are the only conditions that are Baton Passed:

  • Aqua Ring
  • Confusion status
  • Curse status (when used by a Ghost on the Baton Passer)
  • Embargo
  • Focus Energy / other altered critical hit rates
  • Gastro Acid
  • Ingrain
  • Leech Seed
  • Lock-On
  • Magnet Rise
  • Mind Reader
  • Perish Song's count
  • Power Trick
  • Substitute (keeps HP of original user)
  • Stat boosts / drops

Competitive Use

Baton Pass can completely make or break a game in just one move. Passing a stat boost to a sweeper who would normally lack the means to boost its stats effectively can have deadly consequences. For the most part, offensive boosts are Baton Passed out on teams not centered around the attack. Gorebyss and Huntail are renowned for being the only two Pokemon in existence (bar Smeargle) to be able to Baton Pass Shell Smash boosts. A Shell Smash Baton Pass to a teammate like Garchomp virtually seals the opponent's fate. Venomoth, an all-around mediocre Pokemon on paper, is saved from obscurity with its ability to put opponents to sleep with Sleep Powder and quickly Baton Pass Quiver Dance boosts. Mew can Baton Pass boosts of nearly any kind and can come in and pass almost always successfully with its 100 / 100 / 100 defenses and access to Taunt. Some teams even base their entire strategy around Baton Pass, constantly boosting and passing to other teammates until the final pass to a nasty sweeper at full power.
